## The Degree Ceiling Law

For a perspective family f(x) + P = αᵐ:

**deg(x) ≤ [Q(α):Q] · deg(f)**

This single formula explains every degree in the registry.

## The Tight Realization Obstruction (Theorem D)

If d·n = m (tight realization), then d | m. Therefore:

- **3 ∤ 2ᵏ**: Ternary families CANNOT tightly realize binary-degree constants (π, γ, ln(2))
- **2 ∤ 3ᵏ**: Binary families CANNOT tightly realize ternary-degree constants (e, ζ(3), √π, etc.)

**The world classification is forced by prime factorization, not by choice of family.**
